Introduction:

Education is a tool that helps individuals to understand their rights and responsibilities by providing them with knowledge, skill, method, and information. Studying at university fosters creative and independent thought, thus choosing the appropriate university that fits one’s objectives and interests is critical. Selecting a university is one of the most significant decisions a person will make in their life, and it is a problem that many teens confront across the United States and across the World, according to a study by Dianna Sawyer. Colleges and universities in the United States provide students with life-changing possibilities. Choosing to go to college is a big economic decision; choosing your college major, it turns out, is an ever bigger one. The data of total enrollment has been collected from the year 2000 and we have made a projection and prediction till 2029. We infer that around 19.7 million students have attended colleges and universities in Fall 2020, with 16.7 million students attending undergraduate majors. We have projected a steady increase in student enrollment and is expected to grow over 20 million in the year 2029. While joining any school, people look at its placement rate, how much alumni make on average while considering other options and factors. Post-education salary is a factor that needs to be taken into consideration while enrolling in different majors of a university/college and setting a goal in life.

Objective:

One hypothesis which finds support in few of the studies— is that colleges do a poor job of educating students about their major options. And so a question: If students knew more about the average salaries associated with different courses of study, would this influence their major preferences? Students should be aware that the starting pay varies based on the type of school and major that they are enrolled in. Our comprehensive guide to university education in the United States can help in selecting the best university based on the average salaried earned and the students preferences.

    Data Description:

    The dataset that has been used to visualize is from the Wall Street Journal based on data from PayScale. The dataset not only has just the starting and the median salary range, but also various percentile changes of the salaries between the starting and mid-career median pay. The mid-career median salaries are visualized over 10th,25th,75th, and 90th percentiles. In our dataset we have 50 undergraduate major 250 colleges divided into different college type and 320 unique colleges based on their location/region . We have used three files to visualise:

  • Degrees that payback
  • Salaries by college type
  • Salaries by region

    • The visualisations are created with Tableau and a web-based tool called flourish studio, which is a data visualisation platform that creates interactive visuals and conclusions.

    Data Preprocessing:

  • We had to find each university's and college's address, latitude, and longitude. So that they can be depicted on a map of the United States. We used the Geocode add-in for this, which allowed us to use the Google Maps API to get the precise locations for each learning institution.
  • We did delete the columns from the data that had too many missing values. We ended up eliminating the 'Mid-Career 10th Percentile Salary' and 'Mid-Career 90th Percentile Salary' columns since they had more than 50% missing values. We forecasted the enrollment levels for the next eight years using data from total enrolment over the years.
